Checking and approval

An important part of any publication process is the checking and approval for publication. Normally the publisher carries the responsibility for ensuring that each publication is checked and approved according to their standards. One of the first aspects of the checking is the checking of the content. This is normally done by people who are capable of analyzing and understanding the content. Is it complete? Is it easy to understand? Does it communicate well?

A second part of the checking and approval process will review the formatting and page presentation of the content. This process will look at issues such as page size, margins, headers, page numbers, etc. Each element must be properly formatted and placed correctly on each page. All these editing processes require people with a lot of attention to detail.

In any automated preparation process, there is the possibility that some of the content is lost. So the checking process also ensures that all the content approved in the first stage is included and that none has been lost. Some publication processes have limits on the resulting size of the publication. For example, certain types of bindings have a maximum number of pages that can be effectively bound.

Normally, at each step in the publication process checking and approval are required. Certainly after the last step and before the actual production begins, it is necessary to take a last look at all these issues.
