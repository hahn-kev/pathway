sous-gorge nf Throat latch. Thin strap which is part of the bridle (bride) and connects the two sides of the headcrown (têtière) across and under the horse's cheeks and jaws; it is hanging loose and does not have a practical function other than to prevent the bridle from being pulled forwards and upwards. wh: bride.
simple adj Basic, typically when speaking of the type of bridle (bride) or bit (mors), and usually associated with inexperienced riders or young horses. comp. bride simple (see under bride) comp. filet simple 
siège nm Seat. Lowest part in the middle of the saddle (selle) where the rider is sitting. wh: selle 1.

selle nf 1) Saddle. A seat, usually made of leather, which is adapted to the shape of the horse's back for the rider to sit on. pt: troussequin, quartier, faux quartier, petit quartier, sangle, pommeau, siège, étrier 2. 2) The riding style chosen by the rider and for which there may be a distinct tack (harnachement) and different options of sport, and which best suits a particular horse's training. 
seller (der.) v Putting the saddle on the horse's back and tying the girth (sangler). Pour bien seller, il faut un peu de pratique. Le cavalier selle le cheval en vue de l'entraînement. ant: desseller.
desseller (der.) v Taking the saddle off the horse's back, typically once the riding session is over. Une fois de retour à l'écurie, le cavalier desselle son cheval. ant: seller.
sellé (der.) adj Has the saddle osn its back and is ready to be ridden, when speaking of a horse. 
sellerie (der.) nf Room in the stable where the saddles and bridles are kept. 
tapis de selle (comp.) nm Saddle pad. Pad made of sheepskin or some other kind of soft material which is placed before the saddle on the horse's back for more comfort; it is considered an essential piece of equipment. 
selle anglaise (comp.) nf 1) English saddle. Light saddle with a low pommel (pommeau) and iron stirrups (étriers). 2) Style of riding which includes leisure rides, jumping, dressage. 
selle d'amazone (comp.) (sp. var. selle amazone) nf Side saddle. Modified English saddle with only one stirrup (étrier) and which is designed for lady riders to sit on with both legs on the same side. 
selle de course (comp.) nf Racing saddle. Very small and light saddle with very short stirrups (étriers) used essentially for racing. 
selle de dressage (comp.) nf Dressage saddle. Modified English saddle with longer saddle flaps (quartiers) and stirrups (étriers), and a lower seat (siège) to allow the rider to sit closer to the horse and have more contact with their legs on its flanks for subtle weight shifts and precise commands. 
selle d'obstacle (comp.) nf Jumping saddle. Modified English saddle used essentially to ride on obstacle courses and in show jumping, on which the knee pads (matelassure) are relatively thick and the cantle (troussequin) is high to help the rider's balance as the horse leaps over the jumps.
selle mixte (comp.) nf English saddle with combined properties for maximum versatility to fit many disciplines (but none at a high level) typically used in riding schools or stables offering trail rides. 
selle western (comp.) nf Western saddle. Heavy saddle with leather stirrups (étriers) and a high pommel (pommeau) called the 'horn' and which is used for barrel racing, cattle herding, trail rides, and other disciplines. Usually considered to be the opposite of the English saddle. 
contre-sanglon
sanglon nm On the girth (sangle), short strap with a buckle (boucle) at the end which is buckled to the girth tabs (contre-sanglons) on the saddle (selle).
contre-sanglon (comp.) nm Girth tabs. One of the three straps hanging from between the saddle flap (quartier) and the sweat flap (faux quartier) and on which the girth (sangle) is attached. 
sangle nf Girth. Relatively large strap, usually made of leather around the horse's girth (passage de sangle) connecting both sides of the saddle (selle) to hold it tightly on the horse's back. wh: selle 1.
sangler (der.) v Tying the girth as the last step to saddling (seller) the horse. Revenez à gauche, saisissez la sangle et sanglez en commençant par le contre-sanglon antérieur ; ne sanglez ni trop fort ni trop brutalement. ant: dessangler.
dessangler (der.) v Unbuckling the girth, as a step in taking the saddle off the horse's back (desseller). Relevez les étriers, dessanglez à gauche. ant: sangler.
ressangler (der.) v Tightening the girth once the horse has warmed-up to make sure it is not loose and the saddle will not slip down one side; this is important, as most horses harden their belly as a reflex to being girthed. 
fourreau de sangle (comp.) nm Piece of soft material such as sheep skin in which the girth is insterted, both to reduce wear and tear on the girth and to avoid the leather rubbing on the horse's skin. 
passage de sangle (comp.) nm Girth. Part of the horse on which the girth goes, i.e. on the front part of the belly, right behing the front legs. 
desserrer la sangle (colloc.) v Untightening the girth slightly by sliding the buckles on the girth straps (sanglons) a few holes down when the horse shows signs of discomfort or when the training is over and the horse is being walked to cool down. ant: serrer la sangle.
serrer la sangle (colloc.) v Tightening the girth before mounting the horse or after the warm-up to make sure the saddle (selle) won't slide down one side during the riding session. ant: desserrer la sangle.
